For my final project visualization, I created a usability test to see how effective the changes I meant to make for this project would be. I user-tested three people and asked the following questions:

  • While clicking through the test did you find anything confusing?
  • Is there an interaction/ button that you think is missing?
  • Do you think you had enough user feedback?
  • What aspects made this game feel like an actual game?
  • Is there something else you think I should add to make it better?

WHAT USERS SAID

  • The duration of the initial page was too long, which made them wonder if they needed to do anything to access it (like clicking).
  • Maybe the pop-up with instructions could also benefit from having an X-button.
  • The last page could have a “quit” button that leads to the main page and the “start new game” button could lead to the start of a game page.
  • The buttons themselves could benefit from a hover effect that lets users know the button is clickable.
  • The start and ending pages of the game were classic of game design.
  • In terms of what could be added they said animation of sorts, maybe making the coin spin, and sound effects when clicking something.

SUMMARY

From watching the users test my game, I relaized the yeah maybe the after delay on the initial page is too long, which makes users think they have to interact somehow. I also saw the not knowing if to click a button because there wasn’t anything telling them they cold (like a hover effect). In general I think everything my users said about my test were spot on, and I feel like there is a lot of room for improvement, and I should focus more on perfecting the feedback users get.
